About Us

Mission and Vision

Our Purpose

We exist to LEAD the lost to Jesus, teaching them to LEARN what's in the heart of God. Teaching them to LISTEN for their individual's calling. For through these, they will LIVE pleasing God with LOVE to one another.

Our Mission

To glorify God by spreading the gospel through evangelism, fostering authentic fellowship, leading others in passionate worship, equipping believers through discipleship, and serving the community through active ministry.

Our Vision

To be a vibrant community of believers committed to evangelizing the lost, growing together in fellowship, worshiping God in spirit and in truth, making disciples of all nations, and impacting our world through Christ-centered ministry.

What We Believe

16 Fundamentals of Faith

1. Inspiration of the Scripture

We believe that the Scriptures, both Old and New Testaments, are inspired by God and are the revelation of God to man. They are infallible and authoritative in matters of faith and conduct.

2 Timothy 3:16-17 (NIV) “All Scripture is God-breathed and is useful for teaching, rebuking, correcting and training in righteousness, so that the servant of God may be thoroughly equipped for every good work.”

2. The Godhead

We believe in one God, eternally existent in three persons: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.

Matthew 28:19 (NIV) “Therefore go and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit.”

3. The Divinity and Humanity of the Lord Jesus Christ

We believe in the true divinity and humanity of Jesus Christ. He is fully God and fully man.

John 1:14 (NIV) “The Word became flesh and made his dwelling among us. We have seen his glory, the glory of the one and only Son, who came from the Father, full of grace and truth.”

4. The Divinity and Personality of the Holy Spirit

We believe that the Holy Spirit is a divine person, co-equal with the Father and the Son.

John 14:26 (NIV) “But the Advocate, the Holy Spirit, whom the Father will send in my name, will teach you all things and will remind you of everything I have said to you.”

5. Creation and Fall

We believe that God created the heavens and the earth and that man was created in God's image. Through Adam's disobedience, sin entered the world, and mankind fell from grace.

Genesis 1:27 (NIV) “So God created mankind in his own image, in the image of God he created them; male and female he created them.”
Romans 5:12 (NIV) “Therefore, just as sin entered the world through one man, and death through sin, and in this way death came to all people, because all sinned.”

6. Salvation by Grace through Faith

We believe that salvation is by grace through faith in Jesus Christ. It is not earned by works, but is a gift of God to those who believe.

Ephesians 2:8-9 (NIV) “For it is by grace you have been saved, through faith—and this is not from yourselves, it is the gift of God—not by works, so that no one can boast.”

7. Justification, Sanctification, and Glorification

We believe that believers are justified by faith, sanctified by the work of the Holy Spirit, and will be glorified in the final resurrection.

Romans 5:1 (NIV) “Therefore, since we have been justified through faith, we have peace with God through our Lord Jesus Christ.”
1 Thessalonians 4:3 (NIV) “It is God’s will that you should be sanctified: that you should avoid sexual immorality.”
Romans 8:30 (NIV) “And those he predestined, he also called; those he called, he also justified; those he justified, he also glorified.”

8. Water Baptism

We believe that water baptism by immersion is a public declaration of faith in Jesus Christ and an act of obedience to His command.

Matthew 28:19 (NIV) “Therefore go and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit.”

9. Baptism of the Holy Spirit

We believe that the baptism of the Holy Spirit is a subsequent experience to salvation, empowering believers for service and witness.

Acts 1:8 (NIV) “But you will receive power when the Holy Spirit comes on you; and you will be my witnesses in Jerusalem, and in all Judea and Samaria, and to the ends of the earth.”

10. The Church

We believe that the Church is the body of Christ, consisting of all believers, and is called to be a witness to the world.

1 Corinthians 12:27 (NIV) “Now you are the body of Christ, and each one of you is a part of it.”

11. Holy Communion

We believe that Holy Communion is a symbolic act of remembrance of Christ’s sacrifice on the cross.

1 Corinthians 11:24-25 (NIV) “...and when he had given thanks, he broke it and said, ‘This is my body, which is for you; do this in remembrance of me.’ In the same way, after supper he took the cup, saying, ‘This cup is the new covenant in my blood; do this, whenever you drink it, in remembrance of me.’”

12. Divine Healing

We believe that divine healing is provided for in Christ’s atonement and is available to all believers.

Isaiah 53:5 (NIV) “But he was pierced for our transgressions, he was crushed for our iniquities; the punishment that brought us peace was on him, and by his wounds we are healed.”

13. The Rapture of the Church and the Second Coming

We believe in the pre-tribulation rapture of the Church, where believers will be caught up to meet the Lord in the air before the great tribulation. We also believe in the second coming of Jesus Christ to establish His millennial reign on earth.

1 Thessalonians 4:16-17 (NIV) “For the Lord himself will come down from heaven, with a loud command, with the voice of the archangel and with the trumpet call of God, and the dead in Christ will rise first. After that, we who are still alive and are left will be caught up together with them in the clouds to meet the Lord in the air.”
Revelation 19:11 (NIV) “I saw heaven standing open and there before me was a white horse, whose rider is called Faithful and True. With justice he judges and wages war.”

14. The Millennial Reign of Jesus Christ

We believe in the millennial reign of Christ, where He will rule on earth for a thousand years.

Revelation 20:4 (NIV) “They came to life and reigned with Christ a thousand years.”

15. The Final Judgment

We believe in the final judgment, where the dead will be judged according to their works, and the unrighteous will face eternal punishment.

Revelation 20:12 (NIV) “And I saw the dead, great and small, standing before the throne, and books were opened. Another book was opened, which is the book of life. The dead were judged according to what they had done as recorded in the books.”

16. New Heavens and New Earth

We believe that God will create a new heaven and a new earth, where righteousness will dwell and believers will live with God for eternity.

Revelation 21:1 (NIV) “Then I saw ‘a new heaven and a new earth,’ for the first heaven and the first earth had passed away, and there was no longer any sea.”

Our Faith in Jesus Christ

We believe in Jesus Christ, the Son of God, who was prophesied in the Scriptures. He was born of the Virgin Mary, lived a sinless life, and willingly died on the cross for the redemption of humanity. On the third day, He rose from the dead in fulfillment of the Scriptures, conquering sin and death.

We affirm that Jesus, in His glorified, incorruptible body, appeared to many over a period of 40 days before ascending into heaven, where He is seated at the right hand of the Father. We believe in His promised return, when He will come again in glory to judge the living and the dead, and His kingdom will have no end. In Him, we have salvation, eternal life, and the hope of His glorious second coming.

Children's Ministry

Laying a Strong Spiritual Foundation

Our Mission

Our Children’s Ministry provides a Christ-centered worship experience specially designed to help children grow in their relationship with God. Through worship, biblical teaching, and age-appropriate discipleship, we are committed to laying a strong spiritual foundation in the lives of every child.

Children's Worship & Training

Our Children’s Worship Team is composed of Tweens (ages 11–16) who are being trained to serve as worship leaders and instrumentalists—developing a heart for ministry at an early age. As they grow, they transition into the Young People’s Ministry with the prayer that they will continue serving faithfully in the church.

Unified Sunday School

Following worship, we offer Sunday School classes for ages 1–17. Each year, we select a unified curriculum so that the entire ministry studies the same Scripture or topic weekly, with lessons thoughtfully adapted to each age group.

Our youngest learners (ages 1–3) receive simple biblical explanations paired with engaging activities such as coloring sheets. Older age groups explore the same Scripture in greater depth, with teaching suited to their level of understanding. We kindly ask that a parent or guardian attends with children ages 1–3.

Tween's Ministry

Navigating the Transition Years

Our Mission

Our Tweens Ministry is dedicated to guiding pre-teens (ages 11–15) as they navigate the challenges of growing up in today’s world. Recognizing that this age group is highly influenced by social media and cultural pressures, God raised up a team of five leaders specifically to mentor and support them.

Every Sunday evening, the tweens gather for worship and then break into small groups based on age and gender. In these groups, they discuss real-life issues through the lens of Scripture, learning to trust God and seek His guidance rather than looking to the world for answers.

Through consistent discipleship, meaningful discussion, and fellowship, we have seen lifelong friendships formed, spiritual growth, and lives transformed as these young ones begin their journey toward becoming godly teenagers.

Bible Training Ministry

Equipping Believers for Kingdom Service

Our Mission

Our Bible Training Ministry exists to equip believers with a deep understanding of God’s Word and practical skills for ministry. Participants undergo a structured program covering Systematic Theology, Biblical Theology, and Practical Theology, receiving a certificate of completion upon successfully finishing all courses. This ministry provides intensive, hands-on training to prepare individuals to serve effectively in God’s Kingdom.

Program Structure

Training takes place at the Bethesda Building, where participants stay until they complete the program. The training is divided into two parts:

  • Theory: Includes in-depth lesson discussions covering core theological subjects to build a strong biblical foundation.
  • Practical: Participants are assigned to various outreach activities and ministry works to gain hands-on experience in serving God and others.

Feeding Ministry

Nourishing Body and Soul

Our Mission & History

Our Feeding Program began in 2016 with a vision to care for malnourished children and families in our community. We started with 157 children, ages newborn to 12 years, many so severely malnourished they could not sit alone and had to be carried. Each Saturday, we provided a container of lugaw and 3 packs of milk for each child, asking only that parents pray for God’s provision.

Today, we feed 100–175 children every weekend, including those who are malnourished or have been malnourished, who now receive seven packs of milk, one for each day of the week. We also care for pregnant women who lack proper nourishment, ensuring healthier pregnancies and reducing the risk of malnourished babies—a ministry that has borne visible fruit over the past years.

This program is not just about feeding; it is about ministering to children and families, many of whom have never attended church. We bring the love of Christ to homeless and impoverished families, providing spiritual nourishment alongside physical care. Over the years, some families have even begun attending Happy Church, growing in faith through this ministry.

Our Story

Church History

Salvation & Praise Fellowship began with a small group of believers gathering in a living room, united by a hunger for God's Word and a passion for worship. Over the years, God has faithfully expanded our territory, allowing us to touch countless lives in Ozamiz City and beyond.

From our humble beginnings to our current campus on Bernard Avenue, our story is a testimony to God's faithfulness. Every brick, every service, and every saved soul is a chapter in the greater story God is writing through us.
